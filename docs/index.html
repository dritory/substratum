Substratum

an atlas of where the Standard Model and ΛCDM are under empirical stress, and the non-negotiable benchmarks any successor framework must clear.

σ‑over‑time, all tensions

Each line is one anomaly. Numeric σ is parsed from tension_history; hover for the original wording and the comparison being drawn. Multiple comparisons in the same year (e.g. data‑driven vs lattice SM) appear as separate points on the same line.

Per‑tension detail

Small multiples — one card per anomaly, with the same axes for legibility. Cards link to their source file on GitHub.

Length–energy landscape

Every entry placed on log‑log (ℓ, E) axes by its characteristic_scale (tensions) or regime (benchmarks). Circles are open tensions; squares are benchmark constraints. Color encodes layer and status. Click a marker for full detail.

Benchmark coverage

How the 67 benchmark constraints break down. Kind is the role a benchmark plays (reduction, precision test, principle invariance, forbidden phenomenon, cosmological observable, meta). Evaluator status is how hard it is to actually test a candidate framework against the benchmark in practice.

Framework evaluation

Every framework in frameworks/ scored against every benchmark in benchmarks/. The verdict comes from the per-benchmark evaluator under evaluators/: a closed-form compare for trivial bounds, an N-σ check for measurements with uncertainties, or open when the framework supplies no prediction. Hover any cell for the verdict note and the prediction that produced it.

Per-framework benchmark tally

Stacked counts of how each framework's predictions land against benchmarks/. Open cells are unscored, not failed — a framework that has not yet supplied a prediction cannot be falsified by the benchmark, only ignored by it.

Per-framework puzzle coverage (L1, derived)

Each framework's verdict on every puzzle, derived from the addresses_puzzles edges of its composed mechanisms (the strongest role wins). Closed = at least one composed mechanism declares solves or explains_pattern; partial = only ameliorates; requires_external = only requires, i.e. the framework depends on something else to close it; untouched = no composed mechanism addresses the puzzle at all. This is parallel to the benchmark verdicts above: match-with-measurement vs. claim-with-role.

Coverage by physics category

Where each framework actually says something, and where it stays silent. Categories sorted by how many benchmarks the framework leaves open — the long bars at the top are the physics areas the framework currently doesn't address.

Verdict matrix

Rows are frameworks, columns are benchmarks (sorted by the fraction of frameworks that pass them — left columns are easy benchmarks that most frameworks clear, right columns are discriminating ones).

Puzzle ↔ mechanism network

The catalog as a bipartite graph: every puzzle (▣ squares, coloured by kind) and every mechanism (● circles, coloured by type), with a line for each addresses_puzzles edge. Force-directed layout (Fruchterman–Reingold) pulls strongly-connected pairs together; high-degree mechanisms drift to the centre, single-puzzle mechanisms fan out as leaves. Edge colour is the role: solves · explains pattern · ameliorates · requires. Click any legend item to toggle that mechanism type or puzzle kind; hover a node for its summary.

Compression vs coverage

For every mechanism with at least one closed puzzle: x = new free parameters introduced, y = puzzles closed (solves or explains_pattern). The dashed lines are constant-compression rays. Top-left is the dot-alignment sweet spot — many puzzles closed with few parameters. Marker colour encodes status.

Attention vs progress

Every puzzle as a point: x = how many mechanisms address it, y = fraction of those that claim to solve or explain_pattern (rather than just ameliorate). Colour encodes puzzle kind. Four quadrants tell the story directly: quiet victories (top-left, e.g. black_hole_information); the active battleground (top-right, flavor and neutrino patterns); attention without progress (bottom-right, where the hierarchy and cosmological-constant problems live); and under-targeted (bottom-left, the long tail of recent data anomalies awaiting mechanisms).
